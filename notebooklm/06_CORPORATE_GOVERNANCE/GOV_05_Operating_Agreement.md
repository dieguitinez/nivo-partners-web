# OPERATING AGREEMENT OF NIVO PARTNERS LLC

**A Florida Limited Liability Company**

This Operating Agreement (the "Agreement") is effective as of [DATE], and is entered into by and among the Members identified on the signature page of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE 1: ORGANIZATION

1.1 **Formation**: The Company was formed by filing Articles of Organization with the Florida Department of State on [DATE].
1.2 **Name**: The name of the Company is NIVO PARTNERS LLC.
1.3 **Purpose**: The primary purpose of the Company is to provide high-end computer systems design, AI infrastructure, and related technical services.
1.4 **Principal Office**: The principal office of the Company is located in Hillsborough County, Florida.

## ARTICLE 2: MANAGEMENT

2.1 **Management Structure**: The Company shall be **Member-Managed**. Each Member shall have a vote in proportion to their percentage interest in the Company.
2.2 **Authority**: Members have the authority to bind the Company in the ordinary course of business. Major decisions (as defined in Section 2.3) require a majority vote.
2.3 **Major Decisions**: Major decisions include, but are not limited to:

- Amendment of this Agreement or the Articles of Organization.
- Sale or merger of the Company.
- Incurring debt exceeding $[AMOUNT].
- Admission of new members.

## ARTICLE 3: CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS & DISTRIBUTIONS

3.1 **Capital Contributions**: Members have made initial contributions as shown in the Company's records.
3.2 **Distributions**: Net cash flow shall be distributed to Members at such times as the Members determine, in proportion to their percentage interests.

## ARTICLE 4: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY & SOVEREIGNTY

4.1 **Firm Assets**: All methodology, code frameworks, and operational protocols developed by the Company are Firm Assets.
4.2 **Data Sovereignty**: The Company adheres to the "Sovereign Oath" regarding client data and internal intellectual property as defined in `GOV_03_Ethical_AI_Charter`.

## ARTICLE 5: DISSOLUTION

5.1 **Events of Dissolution**: The Company shall dissolve upon the unanimous consent of the Members or as required by Florida law.
